1.	Institutional Overview
Aarogya Advanced Care Institute (AACI) is a super-speciality tertiary care hospital established in 2018 and located in MVP Colony, Sector 5, Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh, India.
AACI is designed as a focused-care institution specializing in:
•	Interventional Cardiology
•	Neurology and Neurosurgery
•	High-Risk Obstetrics and Maternal-Fetal Medicine
•	Critical Cardiac and Neuro Emergency Stabilization
The hospital does not operate as a general multi-speciality facility. Cases outside the above focus areas are referred to partner institutions under formal referral agreements.
Total Bed Capacity: 180
ICU Beds: 32
Modular Operation Theatres: 5
Cath Labs: 1 Hybrid Cath Lab
________________________________________
2.	Clinical Scope Restrictions
AACI does not provide the following services:
•	Cosmetic surgery
•	Organ transplantation
•	Routine psychiatry admissions
•	Elective general surgery
•	Routine antenatal care for low-risk pregnancies
•	Long-term trauma rehabilitation
Medico-legal accident cases are stabilized and transferred unless cardiac or neurological intervention is required.
________________________________________
3.	Emergency Cardiac Response Protocol – Code 12
Code 12 is AACI’s proprietary heart attack rapid intervention system.
Definition:
Code 12 is activated when a patient presents with symptoms suggestive of acute coronary syndrome.
Mandatory Timeline Requirements:
•	ECG within 3 minutes of arrival
•	Cardiac enzyme testing initiated within 8 minutes
•	Cardiologist evaluation within 12 minutes
•	Cath Lab readiness within 20 minutes
Target Door-to-Balloon Time: Less than 45 minutes
If Door-to-Balloon time exceeds 45 minutes, the case is reviewed by the Clinical Audit Committee within 48 hours.
Only the on-call interventional cardiologist may authorize deviation from protocol.
________________________________________
4.	NeuroShield Stroke Grid System
AACI operates under the NeuroShield Stroke Grid for structured stroke classification.
All stroke patients are categorized into:
Grid A – Eligible for thrombolysis
Grid B – Mechanical thrombectomy candidates
Grid C – Conservative medical management
Mandatory Steps:
•	AI-assisted CT brain analysis
•	NIH-based neurological scoring
•	Digital stroke tracker entry
•	Physiotherapy consult prior to discharge
No stroke patient may be discharged without documentation of rehabilitation planning.

5.	Proprietary Clinical Programs
5.1 HeartStrong 360 Program
Eligibility: Post-angioplasty patients treated at AACI.
Program Includes:
•	Three cardiology follow-up consultations within 90 days
•	One dietician consultation
•	Two supervised cardiac rehabilitation sessions
•	One treadmill stress test at 3 months
Program Cost: INR 18,000
Validity: 90 days from discharge
Non-transferable and non-refundable.
Normal cardiology patients not undergoing angioplasty are not eligible.
________________________________________
5.2 Maa Suraksha Platinum Plan
Designed exclusively for high-risk pregnancies managed by the Maternal-Fetal Medicine unit.
Includes:
•	Scheduled fetal Doppler monitoring
•	Dedicated pregnancy care coordinator
•	24-hour emergency direct line access
•	Pre-reserved NICU bed
•	Lactation counseling session
Package Cost: INR 1,75,000
Important Conditions:
•	Covers Cesarean section only
•	Does not cover normal vaginal delivery
•	Valid only for patients registered before 28 weeks gestation
________________________________________
5.3 BrainGuard Post-Surgery Recovery Plan
Applicable to neurosurgery patients discharged from AACI.
Includes:
•	Five physiotherapy sessions

•	One MRI at 40 percent discounted rate

7.	Smart Transparency Billing Model (STB)
All hospital bills are categorized into five structured segments:
1.	Clinical Charges
2.	Procedure Charges
3.	Consumables
4.	Monitoring Charges
5.	Coordinator Charges
Coordinator Charges are unique to AACI and apply to:
•	High-risk pregnancy cases
•	Post-cardiac intervention monitoring
•	Neuro-rehabilitation planning
Pregnancy Coordinator Fee: INR 7,500 (mandatory for Maa Suraksha patients)
Cashless Insurance Policy:
•	Cashless available only for procedures above INR 50,000
•	Below INR 50,000 handled under reimbursement model
Accepted Insurance Providers:
•	Star Health
•	HDFC Ergo
•	FHPL TPA
No other TPAs are accepted.
________________________________________
8.	ICU Operational Policy – Golden 6 Rule
For the first six hours after major surgery:
•	Mandatory 1:1 nurse-to-patient ratio
•	No attendants allowed
•	Continuous monitoring
•	Consultant-level supervision only
If patient instability persists, monitoring extends automatically to 12 hours.

10.	Admission and Referral Policy
Planned Admission Steps:
•	Specialist consultation
•	Diagnostic evaluation
•	Cost estimation
•	Insurance pre-authorization (if applicable)
•	Admission deposit payment
Emergency Admission:
•	Immediate triage
•	Protocol activation (Code 12 or NeuroShield if applicable)
•	Consent from family
•	Stabilization
•	Admission
Referral Policy:
Patients requiring services outside AACI’s specialization are referred within 6 hours of stabilization.
